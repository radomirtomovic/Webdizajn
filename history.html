Early Jazz/New Orleans & Chicago Style Dixieland (1920-1930)

New Orleans style, in music, the first method of group jazz improvisation. Developed near the turn of the century, it was not recorded first in New Orleans but rather in Chicago, Los Angeles, and Richmond, Indiana. Jazz had become popular music in America, although older generations considered the music immoral and threatening to old cultural values.

Important musicians of this era: Louis Armstrong, Bix Beiderbecke, Jelly Roll Morton, Sidney Bechet, Earl "Fatha" Hines
